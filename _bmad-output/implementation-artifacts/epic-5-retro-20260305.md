# Epic 5 Retrospective: Dashboard & Item Discovery

**Date:** 2026-03-05

## 📊 Epic 5 Review

### What Went Well
- **Theme & UI Consistency:** Reusable design system tokens (`theme.colors`, `theme.spacing`, etc.) were consistently applied across the Dashboard, Search/Filter components, and Item Detail screens. The use of `react-native-svg` for the empty state matched the design system perfectly without relying on external assets.
- **State Management:** The decision to heavily utilize the `useItemStore` (Zustand) with derived state computations (`useMemo` for filtering and unique categories) proved highly effective for performance and separating business logic from UI.
- **Architectural Preservation:** In Story 5-4 (Item Detail), the existing complex deletion flow (Firestore + Storage + Store update) from Epic 4 was successfully preserved and wrapped into a new comprehensive detail layout without regressions.
- **Code Quality AI Reviews:** The automated senior developer AI reviews consistently caught edge cases (e.g., whitespace in category filtering, serialized Timestamps from MMKV, and `keyboardShouldPersistTaps`).

### Challenges & Struggles
- **Data Serialization & Type Safety (MMKV vs Firestore):** A recurring theme was the friction between Firestore `Timestamp` objects and MMKV's serialized ISO strings. Story 5-1 and 5-4 required specific fixes to handle non-serializable objects and properly parse dates depending on where the data originated.
- **List Performance Nuances:** In Story 5-3, dynamic filtering required the removal of the `getItemLayout` optimization. Additionally, inline arrow functions in `renderItem` and `CategoryChip` created unnecessary re-renders that had to be addressed in code review.
- **Keyboard Handling:** Overlooked `keyboardShouldPersistTaps` on the Dashboard `FlatList` and `ScrollView` caused UX friction requiring a double-tap to interact with chips or items when the keyboard was open.

### Lessons Learned
- **Plan for Mixed Data Sources:** We must explicitly handle data types (like Dates/Timestamps) differently knowing that items might come fresh from Firestore or be hydrated from local MMKV storage.
- **UI List Scalability:** Dynamic lists (where item counts change rapidly via filter) need different optimization strategies than static lists. `React.memo` is easily defeated by inline callbacks, requiring strict adherence to `useCallback`.

## ⏭️ Epic 6 Preparation (Offline Support & Sync)
Epic 6 focuses on "Offline Support & Sync" (Stories 6-1, 6-2, 6-3).
- **Critical Path:** Epic 6 inherently relies on our MMKV/Zustand local store setup.
- **Dependency/Blocker Risk:** The data serialization struggles identified in Epic 5 (Timestamps vs ISO strings) present a high risk for Epic 6 where local persistence will be the primary source of truth. We must solidify our serialization strategy.
- **Preparation needed:** We need a unified strategy for handling the queueing of Firestore operations when offline, identifying how we track `syncStatus` transitions safely.

## ✅ Action Items for Epic 6
1. **Standardize Date/Time Serialization:** Establish a universal pattern for storing and retrieving dates between Firestore and MMKV to prevent crashes and UI inconsistencies. (Owner: Dev Team)
2. **Review Keyboard Handling Patterns:** Audit all screens with text inputs to ensure `keyboardShouldPersistTaps="handled"` is used on scrollable containers. (Owner: Dev Team)
3. **Queue Architecture Spike:** Before starting 6-1, define the data structure for the background sync queue to handle offline mutations cleanly. (Owner: Architecture)
